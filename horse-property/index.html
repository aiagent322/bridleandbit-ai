What to Look for in Arizona Horse Property

Arizona's year-round riding climate makes it one of the top states in the country for equestrian property. But not all horse property is created equal. Here's what experienced buyers prioritize:

  • Zoning that allows horses — county islands and rural zoning are key in the Phoenix metro
  • Water access — well depth, water rights, and irrigation matter in the desert
  • Usable acreage — flat, fenced land with room for arenas, turnouts, and barns
  • Arena and barn condition — covered arenas, pipe corrals, and wash racks add significant value
  • Trail access — proximity to state land, regional parks, and riding trails
  • Proximity to feed stores, vets, and farriers — rural doesn't have to mean remote

Popular horse property areas include Cave Creek, Wickenburg, Queen Creek, San Tan Valley, Scottsdale, Prescott, Sonoita, Tucson, and the East Valley communities with horse-friendly zoning.
