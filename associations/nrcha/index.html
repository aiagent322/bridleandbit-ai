About NRCHA

The reined cow horse traces its lineage directly to the working animals of California's vaquero tradition — horses bred and trained with extraordinary precision to handle cattle on vast ranches across the 18th and 19th centuries. As the need for that kind of horse faded with the industrialization of agriculture, a group of dedicated horsemen stepped in to preserve it. The National Reined Cow Horse Association — formerly the California Reined Cow Horse Association — was founded in 1949 with a clear purpose: to perpetuate the centuries-long training tradition of the vaqueros and promote the reined cow horse as a legitimate, structured competitive sport.

Today NRCHA is headquartered at 9702 Mineral Wells Highway in Weatherford, Texas, and stands as the governing body for reined cow horse competition worldwide. The association sanctions events across the US, holds clinics and premium media events, and produces some of the most technically demanding equestrian competitions anywhere — events where a horse must demonstrate reining mastery, fence work athleticism, and natural cow instinct, all judged independently in the same class.

"Founded in 1949, the NRCHA was formed with the goal of preserving and educating the public about the rich history of this traditional horsemanship. The NRCHA is responsible for promoting the sport and ensuring high standards of competition."

— NRCHA Mission

The Sport — Three Events in One

What sets reined cow horse apart from every other western performance discipline is the requirement that horses compete in three separate, independently judged events within a single class. A horse that dominates one phase but falters in another cannot win — the sport demands true all-around excellence. Scores from all three events are combined for final placings.

🐄
Herd Work
Horse and rider enter a herd of cattle and demonstrate the horse's ability to hold a single cow away from the herd. Like cutting, the horse works on a loose rein guided only by the rider's body — pure cow sense on display.
Cow Instinct
🌀
Rein Work
A reining-style pattern of large and small circles, flying lead changes, spins, and sliding stops. Demonstrates the horse's athletic ability, responsiveness, and precision in a controlled performance run.
Athletic Control
⚡
Fence Work
A single cow is released and the horse pursues it down the fence at speed, rates it (matches its pace), then turns it back — driving it down the fence in the opposite direction multiple times. The most electrifying event in western performance.
Speed & Power

Major Events

NRCHA Snaffle Bit Futurity®
October · Will Rogers Memorial Center, Fort Worth, TX
The association's flagship event and the most popular reined cow horse competition in the world — restricted to 3-year-old horses competing in snaffle bits. The 2024 Futurity (October 4–18) featured over $900,000 in prize money. The 2024 Open Champion was Chris Dawson (LTE $2.517M). The 2025 Futurity ran October 3–18 with $138,000 in added money plus a progressive jackpot of $34,115+. Also features the Chrome Cash incentive for registered Paint Horses and a premier sale averaging $30,000+ per lot.
$900K+ · 3-Year-Olds · October
NRCHA Hackamore Classic
Annual · 5-Year-Olds
Restricted to 5-year-old horses that have not previously been shown in a bridle down the fence at a judged reined cow horse event. The hackamore (bosal) is the traditional intermediate step in the vaquero progression from snaffle bit to full bridle — the Hackamore Classic honors that training arc, preserving one of the most distinctive elements of California vaquero horsemanship.
Vaquero tradition — 5-year-olds
Tres Osos Cowhorse Derby
Annual · 4 & 5-Year-Olds
For 4 and 5-year-old horses, many of whom competed in the Snaffle Bit Futurity at age three. The Derby is where futurity graduates prove their talent endures as they mature. The 2024 Open Derby Champion was Matt Koch (LTE $1.72M). Also includes the NRCHA Open Western Derby, won in 2024 by Nick Dowers (LTE $1.909M).
4 & 5-Year-Olds
World's Greatest Horseman®
Celebration of Champions · World Championship Show
NRCHA's premier all-around title, contested at the Celebration of Champions — the association's year-end World Championship Show featuring the top-ranked horses and riders. Winning the World's Greatest Horseman title is one of the most prestigious achievements in western performance horse sport. The 2014 champion was Boyd Rice (LTE $1.909M). Also covered on WorldsGreatestHorseman.com.
Premier all-around title
NRCHA Stallion Stakes
Annual · 4 & 5-Year-Olds by Subscribed Stallions
For 4 and 5-year-old horses by stallions subscribed to the event, competing in snaffle bit and hackamore events. Stallion subscriptions drive the breeding economy of the reined cow horse world, with top sires commanding premium stud fees based in part on their Stallion Stakes offspring performance.

Million Dollar Club

NRCHA tracks lifetime earnings for riders, sires, dams, and breeders. The Million Dollar Club is one of the sport's most celebrated milestones — a reflection of sustained excellence across the three-event format that demands complete horsemanship.
