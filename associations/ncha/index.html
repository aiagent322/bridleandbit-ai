About NCHA

The sport of cutting traces its roots to a single afternoon in 1898 in Haskell, Texas, where a horseman named Sam Graves and his 22-year-old horse Hub beat ten other teams before 1,500 spectators to win $150. The goal — a horse separating a single cow from a herd and holding it away on instinct and athleticism alone — hasn't changed in over a century. What changed was the organization built around it.

Founded in 1946 at the Southwestern Exposition and Fat Stock Show, the National Cutting Horse Association is the governing body for the sport of cutting worldwide. Headquartered in Fort Worth, Texas, NCHA promotes and celebrates the cutting horse, whose origins on working western ranches give the sport its authenticity and its soul. The association establishes rules for the conduct of cutting horse shows, maintains a progressive class structure accommodating everyone from beginners to advanced professionals, and produces six major annual events including the prestigious Triple Crown of Cutting.

By 1963, NCHA was already recording results from 727 events — 504 of them recognized as NCHA championship events — and paying out $404,183 in prize money. The Futurity that year paid $23,225. Decades later, a single Futurity now pays out more than $4 million. The 2026 marquee event carries the title of the NCHA Metallic Cat World Championship Futurity, reflecting the breed's tradition of naming its premier event after its presenting sponsor stallion.

The Sport of Cutting

In cutting, horse and rider enter a herd of cattle and select a single cow. Once the cow is separated — or "cut" — the rider drops the reins onto the horse's neck. From that moment, the horse works entirely on its own, anticipating every move the cow makes and mirroring it with explosive athleticism to prevent it from returning to the herd. The rider may not assist the horse once the reins are dropped.

Each run lasts 2.5 minutes. Horses are scored on a 0–infinity scale with 70 as the base score for an average run. Judges reward degree of difficulty — the hotter and harder-to-hold the cattle worked, the better the score potential. Penalties are assessed for the horse losing its cow, the rider picking up the reins, or various other infractions. The sport demands that a horse possess natural cow sense that cannot be manufactured — it either has it or it doesn't.

"The NCHA promotes and celebrates the cutting horse, whose origin on Western ranches allows us to support ranching and its western heritage. NCHA strives to give cutters a level playing field and a progressive class structure which accommodates everyone from the beginner to the advanced competitor."

The Triple Crown of Cutting

NCHA's three premier limited-age events form the most coveted series in cutting — the Triple Crown of Cutting. Each event is restricted to horses within a specific age group, creating a structured path from a horse's debut in competition at age three through its peak performance years.

I
NCHA World Championship Futurity
3-Year-Olds · November–December · Fort Worth, TX
The first jewel and the sport's most prestigious event — restricted to 3-year-old horses that have never been shown in competition cutting. Held at the Will Rogers Memorial Coliseum in Fort Worth since 1962, the same venue that hosted the inaugural event. The first champion was Buster Welch aboard Money's Glo in 1962; Welch went on to win a record five Futurities. The 2024 Open Champion was Kenny Platt aboard Too Hot To Tagg. The 2025 Futurity ran November 10 – December 6, 2025. The 2026 event is the NCHA Metallic Cat World Championship Futurity. The 2021 purse reached $4.16 million with 2,379 entries and $600,000 in added money.
II
NCHA Super Stakes
4-Year-Olds & 5/6-Year-Olds · Spring
The second jewel of the Triple Crown, held each spring. Restricted to 4-year-old and 5/6-year-old horses whose sires are subscribed to the event — making stallion subscriptions a critical part of the cutting breeding economy. The Super Stakes is where horses that dazzled at the Futurity return to prove their talent endures into their four-year-old season.
III
NCHA Summer Cutting Spectacular
4-Year-Olds & 5/6-Year-Olds · Summer · Fort Worth, TX
The third jewel, held each summer in Fort Worth. Features the Derby for 4-year-olds plus Classic and Challenge divisions for 5/6-year-old horses. The 2024 Summer Spectacular 4YO Open Champion was James Payne, who carries lifetime earnings of $5.97 million. Winning all three Triple Crown events with the same horse is one of the sport's ultimate achievements.

Other Major Events

NCHA World Finals
Annual · Fort Worth, Texas
The top 15 competitors in both the Open and Non-Pro divisions from NCHA Weekend Events advance to the World Finals to compete for the NCHA World Championship titles. It is the pinnacle for weekend competitors who build their standings across a full season of local and regional shows.
Eastern & Western National Championships
Annual · Regional venues
Open only to contestants in NCHA affiliate or area standings, the Eastern and Western Nationals provide regional championship crowns across 12 approved classes. NCHA has 25 geographical divisions across the US and Canada, each feeding into these national-level championship events that were split from a single event in 1995.
