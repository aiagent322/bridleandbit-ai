About NRHA

The National Reining Horse Association is the international governing body for the sport of reining, founded in 1966 and headquartered in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma. From a grassroots gathering of western horsemen who wanted to formalize and promote one of ranching's most demanding skills, NRHA has grown into a global organization sanctioning more than 1,140 approved shows per year plus 250 Entry Level events — in arenas spanning North America, Europe, South America, and beyond.

Across all of those events, NRHA pays out nearly $24 million in prizes annually, making it one of the highest-paying equine organizations in the world on a per-discipline basis. Every NRHA-approved judge completes comprehensive training and must pass rigorous testing before receiving judging privileges — guaranteeing that the standard of scoring is the same whether you're competing at a local affiliate show in Arizona or an international event in Italy.

NRHA tracks lifetime earnings meticulously for every rider, horse, sire, and owner who competes under its banner — a tradition that has created one of the most transparent and credentialed competitive records in equine sport.

The Sport of Reining

Reining is a judged event designed to show the athletic ability of a ranch-type horse within a show arena. Competitors must execute a pre-selected, NRHA-approved pattern of maneuvers, each scored on precision, athleticism, and smoothness. Horses are evaluated on a 0–infinity scale with 70 as the base score for an average run — fractions of points are added for exceptional maneuvers and deducted for errors, with penalties for broken patterns or equipment violations.

The sliding stop is the hallmark of the sport — a horse running at full speed and coming to a controlled, sudden halt with its hind legs locked beneath its body, skidding across the ground in a long, smooth track. It is one of the most dramatic maneuvers in all of equestrian sport and the image most associated with reining worldwide.

Sliding Stop
Horse runs at speed, locks hind legs, and skids to a controlled halt — the signature maneuver of reining.
Spins
360-degree rotations done in place, pivoting on a fixed hind foot. Judged on speed, cadence, and correctness.
Circles
Large fast circles and small slow circles, demonstrating control of speed and frame at both tempos.
Flying Lead Changes
Horse changes its leading front leg mid-stride at the lope — executed smoothly between circles.
Rollbacks
After a stop, horse pivots 180 degrees over its hocks and immediately lopes off — no hesitation allowed.
Backup
Horse backs up rapidly in a straight line immediately following the final stop in the pattern.

Major Events

NRHA Futurity
December · OKC Fairgrounds, Oklahoma City, OK
The world's richest reining, restricted to 3-year-old horses. The 2025 edition set a new all-time record with a combined purse of $3.9 million — up 23% over 2024. The Level 4 Open purse reached $1.7 million, with the top individual payout exceeding $350,000. The open finals alone paid out more than $2.7 million. The 2025 Level 4 Open Champions were Casey Deary & Belissimo. The 2025 Level 4 Non Pro Champions were Gina Schumacher & CS Sailor Made.
$3.9M combined purse — 2025 record
6666 NRHA Derby presented by Markel
June · Oklahoma City, OK
The premier event for 4-year-old reining horses. Held each June and widely regarded as the second most prestigious event on the NRHA calendar. The 2024 champion was Cade McCutcheon, who has since surpassed $3 million in NRHA lifetime earnings. The Derby also features rookie and youth divisions, giving the next generation a spotlight at the sport's elite level.
Elite 4-year-old reining championship
Adequan North American Affiliate Championships
December · Held concurrently with the Futurity
The NAAC provides a championship pathway for amateur, Non Pro, Youth, and affiliate-level competitors worldwide. Contested alongside the Futurity at the OKC Fairgrounds, it gives the grassroots competitors — the backbone of NRHA's 1,140+ show circuit — their own world championship moment in the same arena as the sport's biggest event.
Affiliate & amateur world championships
Run for a Million
Las Vegas, Nevada
A high-stakes invitational held in Las Vegas drawing the sport's top professionals to compete for massive individual purses. Cade McCutcheon surpassed $3 million in lifetime earnings with a 6th-place finish here in 2025, earning $60,000 in a single run. The event's format and location have made it one of the most fan-friendly and high-profile dates on the reining calendar.

Million Dollar Club

Reining Horse Foundation

A separate 501(c)(3) nonprofit, the Reining Horse Foundation (RHF) supports the reining community through three core programs. The Dale Wilkinson Memorial Crisis Fund provides financial assistance to reining community members facing hardship — including the Foundation's recent mobilization for members impacted by Winter Storm Fern in early 2026. The NRHyA youth programs offer scholarships and leadership development through the Varsity Reining Club and the annual Buy-A-Pro Auction, giving aspiring reiners access to NRHA Professionals. The NRHA Hall of Fame, located at NRHA headquarters in Oklahoma City, honors the horses and humans who have shaped the sport — from Bill Horn, the first $1 million rider (1995), to the modern era's million-dollar milestones.
