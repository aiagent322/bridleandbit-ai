About the USTRC

Founded in 1989 by Denny Gentry in Stephenville, Texas, the United States Team Roping Championships is the world's leading team roping member organization and one of the largest single-discipline equine groups in the nation. What started as a grassroots effort to give amateur ropers a fair, organized place to compete has grown into a global enterprise with more than 35,000 members across the United States, Canada, Mexico, and South America.

The USTRC built the infrastructure of amateur team roping as we know it today — a numbered handicap system, regional qualifying circuits, and a national finals that draws more competing teams than any other roping event in the world. In 2017, the organization was acquired by Active Interest Media (AIM), which also owns the World Series of Team Roping (WSTR), making AIM the dominant force in competitive amateur roping. Gentry stayed on as Director of Roping Operations, ensuring the culture and competitive integrity he built over nearly three decades remained intact.

The Handicap System

The USTRC runs on a numbered classification system managed by the TRIAD Classification Agency — a separate entity using proprietary software and real-time performance data to assign and adjust every roper's number. Each competitor carries a header number and a heeler number. To enter a division, the combined total of both partners cannot exceed the division cap. A #9.5 team, for example, might pair a 4.5 header with a 5.0 heeler — or any combination that adds up to 9.5 or under.

This system is the backbone of what makes the USTRC work. It puts recreational ropers against other recreational ropers, and prevents sandbagging at every level. TRIAD monitors performance in real time, and numbers can be adjusted based on results — keeping the competition honest from your local jackpot all the way to the National Finals.

The Cinch National Finals of Team Roping

The Cinch National Finals of Team Roping (NFTR) is the crown jewel of the USTRC — and the single largest team roping event in the world by entry count. Held each spring at the Will Rogers Memorial Center in Fort Worth, Texas, the NFTR spans eight action-packed days across three simultaneous arenas: the John Justin Arena, the Pavilion, and the Will Rogers Coliseum.

The three-arena format is an engineering achievement in its own right. A roping team can complete all three of their steers in under thirteen minutes — coiling up in the John Justin, riding into the Pavilion, and finishing in the Will Rogers Coliseum before the adrenaline has even worn off. It keeps the event moving, keeps competitors focused, and creates an atmosphere unlike anything else in the sport.

The 2025 edition — the 36th annual Cinch NFTR — drew nearly 5,500 teams from across the US, Canada, Mexico, and South America, awarding close to $3.3 million in cash plus an additional quarter million in prizes. Champions were crowned in nearly 40 separate events across all divisions, Legends categories, Shootouts, and specialty ropings. The event also hosted the Total Feeds National Breakaway Championships, reflecting the rapid growth of women's breakaway roping nationwide.

The 37th annual Cinch NFTR is scheduled for April 19–26, 2026 at the Will Rogers Memorial Center, Fort Worth, Texas.
