About APHA

It started with one determined horsewoman. On February 16, 1962, Rebecca Tyler Lockhart rallied 16 fellow horsemen to a café in Gainesville, Texas, where they formed the American Paint Stock Horse Association — a registry built to preserve and promote colorful, stock-type Paint Horses. By the end of that first year they had 150 members and 250 registered horses. Registration No. 1 belongs to Bandit's Pinto, a 1959 black tobiano stallion.

More than 60 years later, APHA stands as the world's second-largest equine breed association, with over 1 million horses registered across 59 nations and territories. Headquartered in Fort Worth, Texas, APHA promotes, preserves, and provides meaningful experiences with Paint Horses — from the breeding barn to the show pen, from the ranch to the racetrack. APHA's virtual assistant is even named after its founder, Rebecca, as an ongoing tribute to the woman who started it all from a Texas café with nothing but a vision and sixteen believers.

The Registry & Coat Patterns

Paint Horses are defined by their stock-horse conformation combined with pinto coat patterns — a combination of white and any other color in distinctive patches. APHA registers horses across three primary coat pattern categories, each governed by specific genetics that breeders and owners can explore through APHA's genetic testing resources.

Horses with stock-horse bloodlines but insufficient white markings have historically been registered in the Solid Paint-Bred registry. Beginning January 1, 2025, a landmark rule change — passed with 93.81% of the vote at the 2024 Leadership Gathering — unified both registries for competition purposes. All registered Paint Horses now compete together at APHA-approved events, regardless of coat classification.

Tobiano
Rounded white patches crossing the topline. Legs typically white. Head usually solid-colored. The most common Paint pattern.
Overo
Irregular white patches that rarely cross the back. Includes frame overo, sabino, and splashed white sub-patterns. Often bold facial markings.
Tovero
A combination of tobiano and overo genetics. Horses often display mixed characteristics of both patterns in their markings.
Solid Paint-Bred
Stock-horse bloodlines without sufficient white markings. As of January 2025, compete alongside patterned Paints at all APHA-approved events.
2025 Rule Change

Effective January 1, 2025: All registered Paint Horses — Regular Registry and Solid Paint-Bred Registry — now compete together at APHA-approved events regardless of coat classification. The rule passed with 93.81% of the vote at the 2024 Leadership Gathering in Fort Worth.

Major Events

APHA World Championship Show
June–July · Will Rogers Memorial Center, Fort Worth, TX
The world's premier Paint Horse competition, featuring Open, Amateur, and Youth divisions across reining, roping, ranch horse, hunt seat, and western all-around events. The 2026 World Show features $114,500 added in all-around events alone and 10 world championship roping classes with $11,000+ added. Exhibitor parties, the World of Color Gift Show, and youth activities make it a full-week destination event.
$1.5M+ in awards annually
APHA/WCHA Halter Million
Fall · Fort Worth, Texas
APHA's premier halter horse competition, a joint venture with the World Conformation Horse Association. Features APHA world championship and futurity titles for Paint Horses alongside all-breed classes. One of the richest halter horse events in the country, drawing top breeding stock from across the nation each fall.
$1M+ annually
AjPHA Youth Programs
Year-round · Fort Worth & regional venues
The American Junior Paint Horse Association runs youth-specific competitions, scholarships, and leadership programs alongside APHA's main event schedule. Youth exhibitors compete in all APHA-sanctioned disciplines with age-appropriate divisions for every level of experience.
Scholarships & year-end awards
Leadership Gathering
February–March · Fort Worth, Texas
APHA's annual governance conference where state directors, board members, and general membership convene to vote on rule changes, review strategic direction, and shape the future of the association. Open to all APHA members. The 2025 gathering is where the landmark unified registry rule passed, taking effect January 1, 2025.
Annual governance & rule voting

Publications
