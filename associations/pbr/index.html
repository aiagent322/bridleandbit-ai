About PBR

In 1992, twenty bull riders pooled $1,000 each — some borrowed from family and friends — walked away from the traditional rodeo format, and bet everything on a single conviction: that bull riding, the most popular event in professional rodeo, deserved to stand alone. Founded in Scottsdale, Arizona, the Professional Bull Riders launched with a grassroots operation and an unshakeable belief that no sport could grab an audience the way an 8-second man-versus-beast duel could.

They were right. Today PBR is headquartered in the Fort Worth Stockyards (relocated 2024) and stands as one of the fastest-growing sports organizations in history. More than 800 bull riders from the US, Australia, Brazil, Canada, Mexico, and other countries hold PBR memberships. The organization holds 250+ events worldwide annually and awards more than $15 million in prize money per year. In October 2024, PBR was acquired by TKO Group Holdings — parent company of UFC and WWE — as part of a $3.25 billion deal, cementing the sport's status as a mainstream global entertainment property.

The Format — 8 Seconds

The concept is deceptively simple: stay on a bucking bull for 8 seconds with one hand in the bull rope and one hand in the air. Everything that happens in those 8 seconds — the spins, the kicks, the drops, the recoveries — is judged on a 0–100 point scale split equally between rider and bull. A perfect score is 100 points total. The top bulls are as famous as the top riders — elite bucking bulls are stars in their own right, contracted, insured, and celebrated as professional athletes in their own division.

8 Seconds to glory — or the ground
Clock Start
Bull's shoulder or hip breaks the plane of the gate
Clock Stop
Rider's hand comes out of the rope — voluntarily or not
Disqualification
Free hand touches self, bull, or ground during the ride
Scoring
0–50 for rider, 0–50 for bull — 100 points possible

Tour Structure

Unleash The Beast (UTB)
Elite Premier Series
The top tier of PBR competition and the main path to the World Finals and World Championship. Televised on CBS Sports and CBS Sports Network. Beginning in 2026, Paramount+ becomes the exclusive streaming home of Unleash The Beast under a landmark five-year media rights deal.
PBR Team Series
Team Format
Launched in 2022, the Team Series pairs riders on 10 franchise teams competing in a league format from summer through fall. The Stockyards Showcase at Cowtown Coliseum reached its 100th event in April 2025 — nearly $1.2M paid out at the historic Fort Worth venue by year's end. All 10 teams compete at the 2025 Championship.
Pendleton Whisky Velocity Tour
Primary Qualifier
The most important qualifying series for riders aspiring to reach the Unleash The Beast. Runs in tandem with the premier series throughout the season, featuring emerging talent alongside veterans competing for points and advancement opportunities.
Challenger Series
Proving Ground
Launched alongside PBR Teams in 2022. A proving ground for both affiliated and unaffiliated riders to earn berths into Unleash The Beast events the following season — the proving ground between the developmental level and the sport's elite stage.
Touring Pro Division
Development Tour
The developmental tour where both established veterans and newcomers compete in PBR-sanctioned events while earning points to qualify for the Velocity Tour and ultimately the Unleash The Beast. Runs year-round in unison with the elite series.
PWR — Premier Women's Rodeo
Launched 2025
Launched in late 2025 under Commissioner Linsay Rosser Sumpter. The largest all-women's rodeo — featuring Breakaway Roping, Barrel Racing, and Team Roping with equal payouts across disciplines. Nearly $4.5 million paid to female athletes, and the highest-rated rodeo telecast in history on CBS. Pro, Challenger, and Limited tiers for athletes 16+.

PBR World Finals

🏆
The Richest Bull Riding Event on the Planet

The PBR World Finals is the culmination of the entire season — where the top riders compete for the gold buckle and a $1 million World Champion bonus plus a $350,000 Finals win bonus. For the 2024 and 2025 seasons, the World Finals spanned two venues in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex. In 2026 the Finals return entirely to Fort Worth across two stages.

The 2026 World Finals runs May 7–10 at Cowtown Coliseum and May 14–17 at Dickies Arena in Fort Worth — two venues, one champion. Tickets are on sale now at pbr.com.
