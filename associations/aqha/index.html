About AQHA

Born from a single meeting on March 15, 1940 in Fort Worth, Texas, the American Quarter Horse Association is today the world's largest equine breed registry and membership organization. The idea had been building for years through articles written by Robert M. Denhardt during the 1930s celebrating the history and character of the Quarter Horse — and when horse people finally gathered in Fort Worth, they set in motion an institution that now touches virtually every corner of the horse industry.

Headquartered in Amarillo, Texas, AQHA maintains the official registry of American Quarter Horses, now numbering more than 7 million horses worldwide, with over 430,000 international registrations. Membership stands at approximately 234,000, with more than 32,000 of those from outside the United States. AQHA's mission is to record and preserve the pedigree and integrity of the American Quarter Horse, protect its welfare, promote its versatility, and encourage lifelong enjoyment of the breed — from the show pen to the ranch, from the racetrack to the roping arena.

The early years were not without controversy. Disputes over bloodlines, racing interests, and voting rights led to the formation of two splinter organizations — the American Quarter Racing Association (AQRA) in 1945 and the National Quarter Horse Breeders Association (NQHBA) in 1945. By 1949, all three had merged back into AQHA, consolidating the breed's future under one unified registry that has operated continuously ever since.

"AQHA is the global advocate for the American Quarter Horse, celebrating its legacy, uniting a passionate community and leading the equine industry."

— AQHA Official Mission Statement

The Registry

AQHA maintains a numbered stud book — the definitive, permanent record of every registered American Quarter Horse. It also maintains an "Appendix" registry for first-generation crosses between registered Thoroughbreds and American Quarter Horses. Appendix horses can earn their way into the main stud book by completing extensive performance requirements in racing or show competition and receiving what AQHA calls a Register of Merit (ROM).

Horses produced by artificial insemination or embryo transfer may be registered. Cloned horses cannot. Over 2.8 million horses currently hold numbered registrations, with 430,000+ of those internationally registered — a testament to the Quarter Horse's global reach as a performance and pleasure breed. The registry has operated without interruption since that founding meeting in 1940, making it one of the most complete and reliable equine records in existence.

Sanctioned Disciplines

AQHA sanctions more disciplines than any other equine organization, spanning western performance, English riding, ranch work, speed events, halter, and racing. No other breed association covers this breadth of competition under a single registry.

Major Events

Nutrena AQHA World Championship Show
Oct–Nov · OKC Fairgrounds, Oklahoma City, OK
The world's largest and richest championship horse show. The 2025 edition ran October 30 – November 22, crowning 99 Open world champions, 44 Select World champions, and 85 Level 2 champions across all sanctioned disciplines. The Superhorse title — awarded to the horse earning the most points in three or more events — is the most prestigious single prize in Quarter Horse showing.
$2.3M+ in prizes
AQHA Versatility Ranch Horse World Championships
June · Tri-State Fairgrounds, Amarillo, TX
Combines three premier ranch events: the VRH World Championships, the Ranching Heritage Challenge Finals, and the Cattle Level 1 Championships presented by Four Sixes Ranch. The 2025 event drew 2,470+ entries and awarded over $475,000. The 2026 edition runs June 21–27 with $100,000 added to the payout, the largest addition in the event's history.
$475,000+ in prizes
AQHYA World Championship Show
August · State Fair Park, Oklahoma City, OK
The world's largest single-breed youth championship show, open exclusively to exhibitors 18 and under. No qualifying required for contests. The 2025 edition featured a record $1 million+ prize package, anchored by the new Kimes Ranch Competition Award Fund. Crowns 42 world champions and 33 Level 2 champions annually.
$1M+ prize package
All American Quarter Horse Congress
October · Ohio Expo Center, Columbus, OH
Sanctioned by AQHA and produced by the Ohio Quarter Horse Association, the Congress is the world's largest horse show with 20,000+ entries annually. Held over four weeks in October with 4,000 registered Quarter Horses stabled on site — an industry event unlike anything else in the calendar year.
20,000+ entries annually

Hall of Fame & Museum

Located at AQHA headquarters in Amarillo, Texas, the American Quarter Horse Hall of Fame and Museum preserves and celebrates the horses, riders, breeders, trainers, and contributors who have shaped the Quarter Horse breed and the sports built around it. The Hall of Fame inducted its Class of 2025 at a special celebration held September 12–13, 2025. The museum also hosts rotating exhibits — including the 2024–2025 "Behind the Action: Making Magic with the Horses of Hollywood" exhibit — making it a destination for horse enthusiasts beyond the competition circuit.
