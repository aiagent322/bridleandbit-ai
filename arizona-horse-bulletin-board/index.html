The Bulletin Board Arizona Horse People Trust

The Arizona Horse Bulletin Board is the state's go-to place for equine classifieds. With over 13,500 members, it's where horse people post first when they have something to sell, a service to offer, or a question that needs a real answer from someone who's been there.

Unlike national sites cluttered with dealer ads and scam listings, this is a community-run group managed by Bridle & Bit Magazine. Members know each other, vouch for sellers, and call out bad deals. That trust is what makes it work.

What Gets Posted

The bulletin board covers everything in the Arizona horse world. If it's related to horses and you're in Arizona, this is where to post it:

  • Horses for sale — ranch horses, rope horses, barrel horses, trail horses, broodmares, prospects, and youth horses
  • Tack and equipment — saddles, bits, pads, ropes, boots, blankets, and miscellaneous gear
  • Trailers — bumper pull, gooseneck, living quarters, and stock trailers
  • Hay and feed — bermuda, alfalfa, timothy, and mixed hay by the bale or by the ton
  • Services — farriers, veterinarians, trainers, haulers, barn help, and arena maintenance
  • Boarding and stabling — open stalls, pasture boarding, and training facilities with availability
  • Jobs and help wanted — ranch hand positions, barn manager roles, and riding instructor openings
  • Lost and found — loose horses, missing tack, and community alerts

Why This Group Works

Craigslist is full of scammers. National marketplace sites charge listing fees. The Arizona Horse Bulletin Board is free, local, and moderated by people who actually ride. Here's why 13,500 members trust it:

  • No listing fees — post as many ads as you want, free
  • Local buyers and sellers — Arizona-based members who can see the horse in person before they buy
  • Active moderation — spam, scams, and bad actors get removed fast
  • Real community — members recommend farriers, warn about bad hay, and help each other out
  • Backed by Bridle & Bit Magazine — a trusted name in Arizona's horse industry since 1978
