Why Horse Insurance Matters

A single colic surgery can cost $10,000 or more. A kicked visitor can become a six-figure liability claim. A barn fire can wipe out years of investment in a single night. Horse insurance exists to protect against these risks — but most horse owners don't fully understand what's covered, what's excluded, and what they actually need.

HorseInsurance.ai was built to fix that. It's a free, comprehensive reference covering every major type of equine insurance — written for horse people, not insurance adjusters.

Types of Horse Insurance Covered

  • Mortality insurance — covers death from accident, illness, or disease, typically at the horse's agreed value
  • Major medical & surgical — covers veterinary costs for illness and injury, including colic surgery, laminitis treatment, and emergency care
  • Loss of use — pays a percentage of the horse's value if it can no longer perform its intended purpose
  • Liability coverage — protects owners, trainers, and facilities against third-party injury claims
  • Farm & ranch insurance — covers barns, arenas, equipment, and property against fire, storm, and theft
  • Care, custody & control — essential for trainers, boarding facilities, and anyone who keeps horses belonging to others
