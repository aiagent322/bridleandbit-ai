The East Valley's Go-To Arena

HorseShoe Park sits in the heart of Queen Creek — one of the fastest-growing equestrian communities in the Phoenix metro. The facility hosts events nearly every week, from weekly jackpot ropings to barrel racing series to weekend rodeos. It's the kind of arena where you'll run into the same faces every week and new competitors every month.

The HorseShoe Park Bulletin Board keeps the community connected with real-time event announcements, stabling availability, arena condition updates, and producer schedules. If something's happening at HorseShoe Park, it gets posted here first.

Events at HorseShoe Park

The arena stays busy year-round with events across multiple disciplines:

  • Team roping — weekly jackpots, productions, and weekend ropings drawing headers and heelers from across the Valley
  • Barrel racing — timed-event competitions and barrel racing series with classes for all levels
  • Rodeos — community and sanctioned rodeos featuring bull riding, saddle bronc, steer wrestling, and more
  • Breakaway roping — one of the fastest-growing events in the sport, with regular competitions at the park
  • Youth events — junior rodeos, youth roping, and kid-friendly horse events building the next generation
  • Clinics and practices — roping practices, horsemanship clinics, and open arena time for local riders
  • Special events — benefit ropings, holiday events, and community gatherings throughout the year

Queen Creek's Equestrian Community

Queen Creek and the surrounding East Valley have become one of the strongest horse communities in the Phoenix metro. The area offers what's increasingly hard to find — affordable acreage with horse-friendly zoning close enough to the city for work and school but rural enough to keep horses at home.

The combination of HorseShoe Park as a central venue, a growing population of horse families, and proximity to San Tan Valley, Gilbert, and Mesa has created a community where the western lifestyle isn't a weekend hobby — it's how people live. Kids grow up roping. Families haul together on weekends. Neighbors share hay suppliers and farrier recommendations.

  • Stabling and arena info — stall availability, arena conditions, and facility updates

  • Local services — farriers, vets, hay suppliers, and trainers serving the Queen Creek and East Valley area
