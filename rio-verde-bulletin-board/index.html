Arizona's Elite Horse Ranch Community

Rio Verde sits northeast of Scottsdale in the Tonto National Forest foothills — a landscape of open desert, mountain views, and large-acreage properties built for horses. The area has quietly become one of the premier equestrian communities in the entire Southwest, attracting world-class trainers, breeders, and competitors across multiple disciplines.

The Rio Verde Bulletin Board connects this community with 4,000+ members sharing local events, service recommendations, property news, and neighborhood updates. It's the go-to group for anyone who lives, trains, or does business in the Rio Verde foothills.

A Hub for Every Discipline

What makes Rio Verde unique in Arizona's horse world is the diversity of disciplines operating at a professional level within a few miles of each other. The ranches and training facilities in the area include:

  • Arabian show horses — breeding, training, and showing operations with national and international competitors
  • Reining — NRHA trainers and futurity programs producing top-level reining horses
  • Team roping — practice arenas, jackpots, and hauling-distance access to major ropings statewide
  • Cutting — NCHA training barns working cattle year-round in the mild desert climate
  • Cowhorse — NRCHA versatility ranches combining reining, cow work, and fence work
  • Ranch riding and ranch versatility — growing disciplines with deep roots in the working ranch culture of the area
  • Trail and recreational riding — direct access to Tonto National Forest and miles of desert trail

What Gets Discussed

The Rio Verde Bulletin Board serves the practical needs of the community:

  • Local events — arena ropings, clinics, community gatherings, and ranch open houses
  • Service recommendations — farriers, vets, hay suppliers, fence builders, and arena maintenance who serve the area
  • Property and real estate — homes, land, and horse properties for sale or lease in Rio Verde and surrounding foothills
  • Neighborhood news — road conditions, water district updates, fire season alerts, and community governance
  • Lost animals and safety alerts — loose horses, predator sightings, and community safety notices
  • Boarding and training openings — stall availability, trainer recommendations, and facility updates

Why Rio Verde for Horses

Horse people choose Rio Verde for reasons that go beyond the views:

  • Year-round riding climate — mild winters and manageable summers at slightly higher elevation than the Valley floor
  • Large-acreage zoning — properties built for horses with room for arenas, barns, and multiple turnouts
  • Proximity to Scottsdale — world-class dining, shopping, and airport access without sacrificing the ranch lifestyle
  • Tonto National Forest access — ride out your gate onto public land with hundreds of miles of trail
  • Established equestrian infrastructure — feed stores, equine vets, farriers, and training facilities already in place
  • Privacy and space — a rural feel with none of the HOA restrictions that limit horse keeping in suburban developments
