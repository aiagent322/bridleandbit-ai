The Venue That Defines Arizona Horse Culture

WestWorld of Scottsdale is a 387-acre multi-use facility located at 16601 N Pima Road in north Scottsdale. It's the largest equestrian venue in the Southwest and one of the most important horse show facilities in the United States. From international Arabian shows to NRHA reining events, AQHA shows, rodeos, and breed-specific competitions, WestWorld hosts it all.

The WestWorld community group connects exhibitors, spectators, vendors, and horse people who compete at, attend, or just follow the events that make WestWorld the center of Arizona's equestrian world. Members share event schedules, stabling updates, parking tips, vendor recommendations, and real-time information during show weeks.

Major Events at WestWorld

WestWorld's calendar runs year-round, with the heaviest equestrian season from October through April when the weather brings competitors from across the country:

  • Scottsdale Arabian Horse Show — one of the largest and most prestigious Arabian shows in the world, drawing international competitors every February
  • Sun Country Circuit — NRHA, NCHA, and AQHA-approved shows running through the winter and spring show season
  • Scottsdale Parada del Sol Rodeo — a PRCA-sanctioned rodeo and parade celebrating Scottsdale's western heritage
  • Barrett-Jackson Auto Auction — the world-famous collector car event that takes over WestWorld every January
  • Scottsdale Quarter Horse Show — AQHA-sanctioned competition drawing Quarter Horse exhibitors from across the West
  • Breed shows and specialty events — Paint, Appaloosa, Morgan, and other breed-specific shows throughout the season
  • Clinics and expos — horsemanship clinics, tack sales, and equestrian lifestyle expos

Facilities

WestWorld's scale is what sets it apart from every other venue in the region. The facility includes:

  • Multiple indoor and outdoor arenas — climate-controlled show arenas plus large outdoor warm-up and competition rings
  • Over 2,000 permanent horse stalls — covered stabling with water and electrical access for show horses
  • RV parking and hookups — on-site RV accommodations for exhibitors hauling in with living quarters trailers
  • Vendor and exhibit space — hundreds of vendor booths during major shows and expos
  • Parking for thousands — truck and trailer parking plus general spectator parking across the 387-acre property
  • Food and concessions — on-site dining during events

Tips for WestWorld Visitors

Whether you're showing, spectating, or vending, experienced WestWorld attendees know a few things worth sharing:

  • Book stalls early — popular show dates fill up fast, especially during Arabian week and Sun Country
  • Arrive early for parking — truck and trailer parking can get tight during major events
  • Check the schedule for arena assignments — multiple events often run simultaneously across different arenas
  • Bring layers — the indoor arenas are climate-controlled but outdoor warm-up areas can be chilly in the early mornings during winter shows
  • Follow the community group for real-time updates — gate closures, schedule changes, and stabling alerts get posted by members during show weeks
